\begin{align*}
f(x):=\begin{cases}
x^2 & \text{ if }x \geq 0\\
0 & \text{ if }x <0
\end{cases}\qquad
f'(x):=\begin{cases}
2x & \text{ if }x \geq 0\\
0 & \text{ if }x <0
\end{cases}\qquad
f''(x):=\begin{cases}
2 & \text{ if }x \geq 0\\
0 & \text{ if }x <0
\end{cases}
\end{align*}
we see that the first derivate of $f(x)$ is continuous but the second derivate is not continuous at $x =0$.
